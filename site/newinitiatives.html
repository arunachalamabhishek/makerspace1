New Initiatives

1. Discover and Learn Projects (1‐2‐3‐4 ) In this new scheme launched by IIT Delhi, a group of 4 students one each from first, second, third and fourth year of undergraduate programmes work on a project under the guidance of a faculty mentor. The two basic aims of this initiative is to involve students at a very early stage of their stay at the institute in research and innovation related activities and encourage them to work in a team, jointly with senior students. Second aim of this initiative is to facilitate a continuous operation of research project on a particular objective for at least 4 years in order to produce meaningful output. 2. Student Startup Action initiative is aimed towards supporting student startup activities in the campus by providing a support in terms of recognition, initial seed grant and mentorship. Under the initiative, a group of students can form a team, formulate a proposal and apply for a seed grant. 3. Semester / Year Break for Students to Initiate One’s Own Startup – IIT Delhi has made a provision in its curricula for student to opt for a semester or a year break to start their own startups. This option can be availed any time after completion of four semesters. Students have also an option of earning up to 5 academic credits for this activity. 4. Deferred Placement Options Institute has initiated a deferred placement option for students who would like to continue with the institute beyond their graduation up to 12 months working towards translational research and/or commercialization of their innovations/products. Some of these students are also given pre-incubation fellowship under MHRD’s DIC (Design Innovation Center). 5. Entrepreneurship Development Cell (EDC) The Entrepreneurship Development Cell, IIT Delhi was created with an objective to inculcate and enrich the entrepreneurial environment by creating an easily accessible and exhaustive set of resources for the entrepreneurs, which including the students, the budding professionals, mentors, angel investors and the venture capitalists through various fun-filled yet educating sessions such as Startup Showcases, E-Summits, competitions, eTalks and others. 6. Mandatory Design/Practical Experience (DPE) Credits IIT Delhi in its curriculum has made a provision for students to earn 5 mandatory credits in design and practical experience. Students are given freedom to choose ways in which they can earn these credits as long as their activities have design/innovation content or learning from real world experience. Some representative ways in which students earn these credits include: 1. Specific Courses which promote design and Innovation 2. Optional major design exercises in regular courses 3. Participating Team based product building clubs 4. Design & innovation summer internships within and outside the institute 5. Participation in short-term courses/workshops by design and working professionals 6. Participation in national and international innovation challenges 7. Completing design internships in industries and elsewhere
