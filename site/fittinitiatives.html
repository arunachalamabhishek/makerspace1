FITT Initiatives

FITT Initiatives Related to Student Innovations FITT is an industrial interface organization of IIT Delhi. Its mission is to be an effective interface with the industry to foster, promote and sustain commercialization of science and technology in the institute for mutual benefits. FITT administers and supports innovation activities on campus through various programmes for students and young Innovators. These programs cover some or all phases of innovation like (1) Ideas Stage (2) Proof of Concept Stage (POC) (3) Functional Prototype Stage (4) Incubation Stage. Some of the Industry/Government Sponsored Innovation Programs for students administered by FITT include: 1. ICICI Trinity program is an initiative sponsored by ICICI bank to promote banking innovations from idea stage to functional prototype stage in three different phases. The program conceived by IIT Delhi is open to students from other institutes and universities as well. The program has led to many new innovations in fintech sector. 2. Ericsson Innovation Award (Idea to Incubation) were instituted in 2014 to recognize the spirit of innovation among students at Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs). With these awards, Ericsson aims to facilitate innovative projects from engineering students and seeks to support some deserving ventures all the way through to their incubation phase. 3. Pfizer (IIT Delhi Innovation and IP Program) is a collaborative incubation accelerator co-created by Pfizer and Foundation for Innovation and Technology Transfer (FITT) at the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi (IIT Delhi). The Program provides – funding, resources, expertise and infrastructure – to propel India’s healthcare innovations from idea to IP. 4. Women Entrepreneurship and Empowerment (WEE) is a first of its kind initiative by IIT Delhi to strengthen women entrepreneurial ecosystem which is also supported by Department of Science and Technology, Government of India. WEE intends to focus and ignite an entrepreneurial spirit amongst women from a college going student to a middle aged housewife to embrace entrepreneurship as a viable, fulfilling career option. WEE has created a high powered program designed to address the specific needs, challenges and opportunities faced by women entrepreneurs. The program caters to financial and business fundamentals that every women entrepreneur must know to run her business profitably. It enables chosen women entrepreneurs to understand their business ideas, carve value proposition, and understand customer needs and channels to reach them, product positioning, leadership style development, strategic thinking and specifics of the industry.propel India’s healthcare innovations from idea to IP. 5. POSOCO – Power System Award for best innovation (PPSA) recognizes and rewards fresh Doctoral/Masters research accomplishments in power system and related fields. It aims not only to discover and reward innovative solutions in Power Systems, but also motivate students to undertake further research in the field and ensure that the successful technologies identified in the process can be disseminated for possible industry adoption.
